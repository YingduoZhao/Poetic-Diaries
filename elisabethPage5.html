Empress Elisabeth's Poetic Diaries



on the wind - Kaiserin Elisabeth con österrich



On the waves stands the master,
And his hand beckons to me;
Oh! why am I not more brazen
And leave the ship Rand!

Every day I go out
into the wide, deep blue sea;
Every day I say: "Finally today
I will not return home."

Shall he not wait any longer,
And I plunge down;
And yet my blood freezes
This wet, cold grave!

Can't I reach him!
"Boatman, go there!
Don't you also see the man,
The noble, pale On the sea?"

But the old man constantly says
That he never sees such a thing;
And I think it scared
Him miserably in front of me.

Auf den Wogen steht der Meister,
Und es winkt mir seine Hand;
Ach! warum bin ich nicht dreister
Und verlass des Schiffes Rand!

Täglich fahre ich ins weite,
Tiefe blaue Meer hinaus;
Täglich sprech' ich: »Endlich heute
Kehre ich nicht mehr nachhaus."

Länger soll er nicht mehr harren,
Und ich stürze mich hinab;
Und doch macht mein Blut erstarren
Dieses nasse, kalte Grab!

Kann ich ihn denn nicht erreichen!
»Bootsmann, steure dorthin zu!
Siehst den Mann, den edeln, bleichen
Auf dem Meere nicht auch Du?"

Doch der Alte sagt beständig,
Dass er nie dergleichen schaut;
Und ich glaub', es hat elendig
Ihm dabei vor mir gegraut.



The word "Master" indicated on the first line of the poem refers to Heinrich Heine, a German poet, writer and literary critic. The passion of Elisabeth's life, however, was the great poet Heinrich Heine. Heine died fifty-nine at the time when she was nineteen (1856). Eventhough they never never met eachother, Elizabeth often claimed that the soul of Heinrich Heine joined her at night and dictated the verses to her. Her enthusiasm of studying Heine's work made the Empress a great expert of his work. She was so experted that she could recognize the authenticity of a poem with absolute confidence better than the scholars.

The most fertile period of creativity Elizabeth went from 1884 to 1889. In 1884 she met another queen who, as a poet, it was very popular: her namesake Elizabeth of Romania, the German born Princess of Wied (1843 1916), whose nom de plume was the evocative Carmen Sylva. Though younger than her, the Queen of Romania seemed to her grandmother, as she was prematurely aged, the two women understood perfectly and became great friends. This, added to the cult of Heine, increased the interest of Sissi for poetic composition.
