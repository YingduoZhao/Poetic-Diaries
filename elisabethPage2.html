Empress Elisabeth's Poetic Diaries

Answer To - Kaiserin Elisabeth con österrich




That you did not praise my "call",
yes, that you even found it very bad,
This really did not offend me,
I did not feel the rebuke sting.

In his soul I have sunk.
myself too deeply And too enthusiastically wound the wreath to Him?
Who imposes such criticism on me,
The Poor One barks well with other dogs.

Dass meinem »Aufruf« du nicht Lob geschenkt,
Ja, dass du ihn sogar sehr schlecht gefunden,
Es hat mich dies wahrhaftig nicht gekränkt,
Ich hab' des Tadels Stachel nicht empfunden.

In seine Seele hätt' ich mich zu tief versenkt.
Und zu begeistert Ihm den Kranz gewunden?
Der solche Kritik über mich verhängt,
Der Arme bellt mir gut mit andern Hunden.







In contrast to her other habit of not showing her poems to anyone, Elisabeth read the "appeal" several
times in the family circle, but did not find the expected enthusiasm, but rather restraint. To whom the
following poem is addressed, it is not clear to clarify. The emperor can be the addressee as well as
Elisabeth's younger brother, Duke Carl Theodor in Bavaria, who thought little of too much enthusiasm
for Heinrich Heine. He even warned his sister not to "drill too intensely into the overstretched ideas
in which she lives, because he thinks that through this imaginary soul intercourse with Heine she can
overstimulate her nerves so much that in the end she 'snaps over'" (Hamann, 450).
